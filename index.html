1. Overview

Moving Drawdown Strategy

This strategy is designed to recover from drawdown using a combination of runner trades and quick scalps. The first entries aim for larger targets while subsequent entries take smaller, higher-probability profits.

Parameter Entry 1-2 (Runner) Entry 3+ (Scalp)
Instrument MGC (Micro Gold Futures)
Timeframe 2-minute chart
Profit Target 150-200 ticks 40-50 ticks
Stop Loss 200 ticks 200 ticks
Risk:Reward 1:1 (or better) 4:1 (risk:reward)
Auto-Breakeven Yes (at 70 ticks) No
Auto-Trail Yes (70 ticks) No
üìà Strategy Goal
Use the first 1-2 entries to catch larger moves with trailing stops. If those don't hit, switch to quick scalps to chip away at the drawdown with high win-rate trades.

2. Strategy Concept

Phase 1: Runner Entries (Entry 1-2)

The first two entries are designed to catch larger directional moves:

  • Target: 150-200 ticks profit
  • Wide stop loss (200 ticks) gives room to breathe
  • Auto-breakeven at 70 ticks protects capital
  • Trailing stop locks in profits as trade moves in your favor
üí° Why Runners First?
When in drawdown, one good runner can recover multiple small losses. The trailing stop ensures you capture extended moves while the auto-breakeven protects against reversals.

Phase 2: Scalp Entries (Entry 3+)

After the first two entries, switch to quick scalps:

  • Target: 40-50 ticks profit
  • Same wide stop (200 ticks) for consistency
  • No auto-breakeven ‚Äî let the trade hit TP quickly
  • Higher win rate due to smaller target
‚úì Scalp Advantage
Smaller targets are hit more frequently. Each 50-tick win chips away at the drawdown while the wide stop prevents getting stopped out on normal volatility.

1. Overview

Hold the Runner Strategy

This strategy captures large directional moves of 700+ ticks by riding the trend from signal to signal. Unlike scalping strategies, you hold the position until an opposing signal appears ‚Äî letting winners run to their full potential.

Parameter Value
Instrument MGC (Micro Gold Futures)
Timeframe 5-minute chart
Target Move 700+ ticks ($700+ per contract)
Take Profit None ‚Äî exit on opposing signal
Stop Loss 200 ticks
Auto-Breakeven At 200 ticks profit
Trailing Stop 200 ticks (after BE triggers)
Preferred Session Asia Open or NY Session
üèÉ The Goal: Catch the Big Move
Gold often makes 700-1000+ tick moves over a session. This strategy aims to capture those moves by entering early and holding until the trend reverses. The trailing stop protects profits while giving the trade room to breathe.
üö´ NO TRADING ON NEWS DAYS
Avoid this strategy on days with major economic releases. News volatility can trigger your stop on whipsaws before the real move begins.

5. Cool Down Period

‚è∏Ô∏è Why Cool Down?
After exiting on an opposing signal, do not immediately re-enter in the new direction. The initial opposing signal could be an impulse move or fake-out. Wait for confirmation.

Cool Down Rules

1

Exit on Opposing Signal

Close your position when the opposing signal appears.

2

Wait for Confirmation

Do NOT immediately enter in the new direction. Wait for another signal in the new direction to confirm the reversal is real.

3

Enter on Confirmation

Once you see a second signal confirming the new direction, enter the new trade with the same ATM settings.

Example: Cool Down in Action

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid Impulse Moves
The cool down period prevents you from getting caught in a fake reversal. Many opposing signals are just profit-taking before the original trend continues. Wait for confirmation!
